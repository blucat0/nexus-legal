Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 13, 2026

Plain English version: NexusPanel needs some data to work, especially account, tenant, integration, cached device, and system status data. We do not want secrets exposed publicly, and we aim to keep tenant data separated.

1. What this policy covers

This Privacy Policy explains how NexusHQ and NexusPanel may collect, use, store, protect, and share information when you use our websites, app, integrations, documentation, agents, and related services.

2. Information we may collect

Depending on how you use NexusPanel, we may collect or process:

  • Account information, such as name, email address, password hash, role, login status, and account settings.
  • Tenant and organization information, such as company name, tenant membership, roles, and approval status.
  • Integration settings, such as vendor names, connection status, permission scopes, and encrypted integration secrets.
  • Cached operational data, such as customers, devices, users, licenses, backups, security status, alerts, and system health information from connected services.
  • Agent data, such as heartbeat times, device names, command status, volume state, playback state, and basic system status from approved agent installs.
  • Usage and security data, such as login attempts, IP address, browser info, timestamps, audit logs, errors, and service activity.
  • Support information, such as messages, screenshots, troubleshooting notes, and issue details you choose to provide.

3. How we use information

We may use information to:

  • Provide NexusPanel and related services.
  • Authenticate users and protect accounts.
  • Keep tenant data separated.
  • Connect to integrations you choose to enable.
  • Display dashboards, device lists, alerts, and customer information.
  • Run approved agent features.
  • Debug, maintain, secure, and improve the service.
  • Detect abuse, unauthorized access, or security issues.
  • Communicate about accounts, support, security, approvals, and service updates.

4. Integration data

When you connect third party services, NexusPanel may receive information from those services based on the permissions you grant. This can include device data, customer data, user data, license data, backup data, security status, ticket related data, and other information made available by the connected service.

We only want NexusPanel connected to systems you are authorized to manage. You are responsible for making sure you have permission to connect each integration.

5. Secrets and credentials

NexusPanel may store integration secrets or keys needed to connect services. Those secrets should be encrypted or otherwise protected at rest. We should not display saved secrets back to users in plain text after they are saved.

You should never submit secrets through public GitHub repositories, public screenshots, public documentation, or insecure support channels.

6. How we share information

We do not sell personal information. We may share information only when needed to:

  • Provide the service through hosting, infrastructure, database, logging, security, or email providers.
  • Connect to third party integrations you choose to enable.
  • Comply with legal obligations.
  • Protect NexusHQ, users, tenants, customers, systems, or the public from harm.
  • Handle a business transfer, such as a merger, acquisition, restructuring, or sale of assets, if that ever happens.

7. Data retention

We keep information for as long as needed to provide the service, meet legal obligations, resolve disputes, troubleshoot issues, protect security, and maintain business records. Cached integration data may be refreshed, replaced, or deleted over time.

8. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational steps to protect information. No system is perfect, and we cannot promise that unauthorized access, outages, bugs, or security incidents will never happen. Users also need to protect their own accounts, devices, credentials, API keys, and connected services.

9. Your choices

You may be able to update account information, disconnect integrations, rotate keys, remove users, or request deletion of certain data. Some data may need to be retained for security, legal, audit, billing, or operational reasons.

10. Children

NexusPanel is not intended for children. It is meant for IT, business, and authorized administrative use.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as the product changes. If the update is important, we will try to make that clear.

12. Contact

Privacy questions can be sent through the contact information listed on the Legal Contact page.
