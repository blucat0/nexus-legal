Data Processing Notice

Last updated: June 13, 2026

1. Purpose

This notice explains the types of data NexusPanel may process while providing IT dashboard, integration, monitoring, agent, and workflow features.

2. Types of processed data

NexusPanel may process:

  • Tenant and organization data.
  • User account and role data.
  • Customer and asset data from connected systems.
  • Device names, device status, operating system data, and management links.
  • Microsoft 365 license, domain, user, and tenant information when connected.
  • Security tool status, backup status, and alert information when connected.
  • Sound Agent status, command status, heartbeat data, and device metadata.
  • Audit, login, troubleshooting, and operational logs.

3. Why data is processed

Data is processed to provide the service, display information, run approved actions, maintain tenant separation, troubleshoot problems, detect abuse, secure the service, and improve reliability.

4. Tenant responsibility

Tenant owners and admins are responsible for making sure connected systems are authorized, user access is appropriate, customer permissions are respected, and integrations are removed when no longer needed.

5. Caching

NexusPanel may cache data from third party services to improve speed, reduce API usage, and display dashboards. Cached data may not always be live. For critical decisions, verify details in the original vendor portal.

6. Subprocessors and vendors

NexusPanel may rely on hosting, database, logging, email, security, repository, and infrastructure providers. Third party integrations are also processed through the vendors you choose to connect.

7. Deletion and export

Data deletion and export options may depend on account status, tenant status, technical limits, legal obligations, security needs, backup retention, and connected vendor systems.
